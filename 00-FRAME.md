1. You’re building a local, transcript-first tool for talking-heavy streamers, not a traditional video editor.
2. The core pain is subject sprawl and digression in long streams, not repetition, highlights, or pacing tricks.
3. The tool extracts distinct topics and helps the creator isolate one coherent thread from a long stream.
4. Output is a reversible cut plan and structural suggestions — never auto-deletions, rewrites, or black-box edits.
5. Design principle: suggest, don’t assume; expose structure and options without deciding tone or framing.
6. The tool must avoid “authorial voice”: it should not invent titles, hooks, or rewritten phrasing.
7. Context depth comes from RAG + a creator-controlled Lore/Glossary, not hidden training or stylistic imitation.
8. Strong OSS / open-core ethos: transparent logic, portable outputs, no workflow or data lock-in.
9. Explicit goal is to avoid closed systems that capture creative control or replace creator judgment.
10. Validation target is clarity: “this is the video this stream wants to be”, not polish, automation, or growth.
